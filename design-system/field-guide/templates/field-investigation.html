I

Identification

  • Mark 1 m² with string at ground level
  • Count plant species without picking — use lens for small features
  • Record signs: tracks, scat, chew marks, webs
  • Photograph wide + one detail with scale

Quick facts

LevelBeginner · 30–45 minutes
PlaceBackyard, park edge, or trail margin
MaterialsString or sticks, notebook, hand lens optional
Driving questionWho lives here — plants, animals, signs?
SafetyGloves if soil contact; watch for stinging insects
EthicsObserve only; do not dig or remove organisms
II

Why it matters

Ecology begins at the scale you can actually see. One square meter defeats overwhelm and builds honest species awareness.

III

How it connects

  • Introduces biodiversity metrics used in research
  • Links to soil, moisture, and light microhabitat
  • Foundation for intermediate plot surveys
  • Pairs with WEF Lesson 101 — Observe

VI

Outdoor investigation

  1. BEFORE YOU GO: check weather; confirm permission on private land.
  2. OBSERVE 10 min: sit still. List 5 seen, 3 heard, 1 smelled.
  3. ASK: one question your list raised.
  4. COLLECT EVIDENCE: photo with date and approximate location.
  5. CONNECT: name two relationships between organisms or abiotic factors.
  6. REFLECT: before vs. after — what changed in your understanding?
  7. RETURN: revisit next week; same square if possible.
