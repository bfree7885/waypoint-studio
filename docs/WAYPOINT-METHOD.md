# The Waypoint Method

**Educational philosophy for every Waypoint Studio product**

*Read first:* [`WAYPOINT-STUDIO-CONSTITUTION.md`](WAYPOINT-STUDIO-CONSTITUTION.md) — the Constitution is supreme law. The Waypoint Method is how we teach.

---

## MISSION

### Why Waypoint Studio teaches differently

Most nature apps hand you information and hope you remember it. Waypoint Studio teaches the way Earth Science is taught in the best labs and field courses: **you go first, then you read.**

We exist because people have fallen out of relationship with the living world — not from lack of data, but from lack of *practice*: the habit of looking, asking, testing, and returning.

**Observe. Understand. Create. Share.**

The Waypoint Method is our promise:

- **Observe** before you are told what to see  
- **Understand** connections, not isolated facts  
- **Create** evidence, notes, photographs, and meaning  
- **Share** ethically — with yourself, your community, or science, only when you choose  

Technology is the field laboratory, the specimen cabinet, the map on the trailhead wall. It is not the forest. Every lesson, article, video, species page, prediction, and gallery exists to send you **back outside** with better questions than you had before.

## PHILOSOPHY

### Technology should encourage outdoor exploration — not replace it

| We believe | We reject |
|------------|-----------|
| The screen is a preparation room | The screen as destination |
| Tools extend the hand and eye | Tools that replace the hand and eye |
| Learning is cyclic, not linear | "Complete the module" |
| Evidence beats authority | "Trust the algorithm" |
| Quiet attention | Engagement metrics |
| Optional contribution | Surveillance disguised as science |

**The lab teacher's rule:** If a student finishes a lesson and stays indoors, the lesson failed.

**The field guide's rule:** Name what can be seen with the naked eye before what requires a microscope, a subscription, or a server.

**The photographer's rule:** A frame is a record of attention. Document what you noticed, when, and where — beauty follows honesty.

## THE WAYPOINT LEARNING CYCLE

The complete cycle from observation to understanding. Every lesson, article, video, and tool interaction should move learners through this loop — and the loop always opens again outdoors.

```
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                          │
    ▼                                                          │
 OBSERVE ──► ASK QUESTIONS ──► INVESTIGATE ──► USE THE TOOL   │
    │              │                │              │           │
    │              │                │              ▼           │
    │              │                │         COLLECT EVIDENCE │
    │              │                │              │           │
    │              │                ▼              ▼           │
    │              │           CONNECT IDEAS ◄─────┘           │
    │              │                │                          │
    │              │                ▼                          │
    │              │           REFLECT                          │
    │              │                │                          │
    └──────────────┴──────────────── GO BACK OUTSIDE ──────────┘
```

### Stage 1 — Observe

**What happens:** The learner encounters a real phenomenon — in nature, in a photograph, in a map, in a video frame — before receiving an explanation.

**In content:** Open with an image, a question, or a field note. Never open with a definition.

**Prompt:** *What do you notice without being told what to look for?*

---

### Stage 2 — Ask questions

**What happens:** Curiosity is named and honored. Wrong questions are welcome.

**In content:** A single driving question per lesson. Sub-questions for investigation.

**Prompt:** *What do you wonder? What would you need to see to answer it?*

---

### Stage 3 — Investigate

**What happens:** Background knowledge arrives as context for inquiry — not as trivia. Illustrations, diagrams, maps, and short video support the question.

**In content:** Background, mechanisms, identification keys, comparative photos.

**Prompt:** *What do experts look for — and what can you look for where you live?*

---

### Stage 4 — Use the tool

**What happens:** The product's workshop (prediction, animation, mapping, logging, identification) is applied to **the learner's own evidence or scenario** — not a demo that ends on screen.

**In content:** Guided tool steps tied to the lesson question. "Try this with today's photograph / your location / your journal entry."

**Prompt:** *What does the tool help you see that your eye alone might miss?*

---

### Stage 5 — Collect evidence

**What happens:** The learner gathers data: a photo, a measurement, a sketch, a species ID attempt, a weather reading, a journal paragraph.

**In content:** Field observation checklist; metadata (date, place, conditions); photography as documentation.

**Prompt:** *What is your evidence? Could someone else understand what you saw?*

---

### Stage 6 — Connect ideas

**What happens:** The phenomenon is placed in the web of geology, weather, life, water, soil, and human presence.

**In content:** Connections section (required). Cross-links to species, habitats, lessons, News.

**Prompt:** *What else had to be true for this to happen?*

---

### Stage 7 — Reflect

**What happens:** The learner articulates what changed in their understanding — and what remains uncertain.

**In content:** Reflection prompts in Field Notes; no single "correct" essay.

**Prompt:** *What surprised you? What would you check next time?*

---

### Stage 8 — Go back outside

**What happens:** An outdoor investigation, challenge, or repeat observation is assigned. The cycle restarts.

**In content:** Field investigation template (below); seasonal challenge; "return in one week and note the difference."

**Prompt:** *When will you go? What will you bring?*

## LAB STRUCTURE

Every Waypoint lesson is a **digital field lab**. Whether it appears in Learn, a species page, an article, or a video companion, it should include these elements when relevant (skip with reason only when truly inapplicable):

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Question** | One driving question — the reason for the lab |
| **Background** | Just enough context to investigate; cite phenomena, not credentials |
| **Illustrations** | Watercolor plates, ink diagrams, Peterson-style figures |
| **Photography** | Evidence frames — wide, detail, comparison, scale |
| **Maps** | Where this happens; range; aspect; geology; watershed |
| **Diagrams** | Process, cycle, cross-section, key, timeline |
| **Videos** | Demonstration or habitat walk — curated, click to play, never autoplay |
| **Field observations** | What to notice with senses on site |
| **Use the tool** | Product workshop step tied to the question |
| **Outdoor investigation** | Structured task outside (see template below) |
| **Reflection** | What you thought before vs. after |
| **Related lessons** | Reading order across tracks and products |

**Alignment with WEF:** The Waypoint Education Framework (`design-system/education/`) implements this lab structure in software. The Waypoint Method is the philosophy; WEF is the canonical lesson schema. The [**nine-pillar Educational Framework**](WAYPOINT-EDUCATIONAL-FRAMEWORK.md) is the company-wide topic standard that WEF lessons, field guides, and content types map onto.

## FIELD INVESTIGATIONS

### Reusable outdoor investigation template

Every investigation uses the same skeleton. Difficulty changes scope, precision, and independence — not the shape of inquiry.

```
INVESTIGATION TITLE
─────────────────────
Driving question:
Place (your site):
Season / date:
Estimated time:
Materials:

BEFORE YOU GO
  • Safety and ethics
  • Permission (land, species, privacy)
  • Weather / conditions check

OBSERVE (10–20 min)
  • Sit still. No phone except to record.
  • List 5 things you see, 3 you hear, 1 you smell.
  • Photograph ONE subject as evidence (wide + detail).

ASK
  • Write one question your observations raised.

INVESTIGATE
  • Use the lesson, map, or tool to form a hypothesis.

COLLECT EVIDENCE
  • Measure, sketch, or photograph with metadata:
    date · time · location · weather · habitat

CONNECT
  • Name two connections (e.g. slope + soil + plants; temperature + insects).

REFLECT
  • What did you think before? What do you think now?
  • What would you do differently next visit?

OPTIONAL — CITIZEN SCIENCE
  • If contributing: what data, where it goes, your choice to share.

RETURN
  • Schedule a repeat visit (tomorrow / next week / next season).
```

### Beginner investigations

*Short, local, one sense or one species. 30–45 minutes. Backyard, park, or window.*

| Title | Driving question | Focus |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| **The One Square Meter** | Who lives in one meter of ground? | Ecology · observation |
| **Cloud Names** | What is the sky doing today? | Weather · photography |
| **Same Tree, Four Sides** | Does the bark change around the trunk? | Plants · microhabitat |
| **Morning Sound Map** | Where are birds concentrated? | Wildlife · sound |
| **Shadow Length at Noon** | Where is the sun on its arc? | Astronomy · season |
| **After Rain** | What changed in smell, color, and movement? | Weather · reflection |

---

### Intermediate investigations

*1–2 hours. Compare two sites or two times. Notes + photos + simple map.*

| Title | Driving question | Focus |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| **Slope vs. Slope** | How does aspect change plants on two hillsides? | Ecology · geology |
| **Water's Edge** | Where does the bank transition from wet to dry plants? | Hydrology · habitats |
| **Track and Sign** | What passed here without being seen? | Wildlife · field skills |
| **Fog Line** | At what elevation does fog settle today? | Weather · topography |
| **Pollinator Watch** | Who visits the same flower for 15 minutes? | Ecology · insects |
| **Rock Story** | What three clues tell this stone's history? | Geology · photography |

---

### Advanced investigations

*Half day or return visits. Data table, GPS optional, research brief connection.*

| Title | Driving question | Focus |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| **Phenology Transect** | How does bud burst change along an elevation gradient? | Climate · plants · citizen science |
| **Watershed Walk** | Where does this water go after it leaves your sight? | Hydrology · maps · conservation |
| **Night Sky Inventory** | What can you document about light pollution and visibility? | Astronomy · conservation |
| **Invasive vs. Native Patch** | What patterns distinguish managed from wild edges? | Ecology · ethics |
| **Soil Horizons Pit** | What layers appear in a safe, permitted cut bank? | Geology · soils |
| **Repeat Photography** | What changed in this frame since last season? | Photography · climate · journal |

## PHOTOGRAPHY

### Photography is evidence

Waypoint Studio teaches photography as **scientific and personal documentation** first, aesthetic achievement second. A beautiful image that lies about conditions, disturbs wildlife, or erases context fails the Method.

**Teach learners to record:**

| Element | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Subject** | What is the evidence about? |
| **Context** | Wide shot — habitat, weather, scale |
| **Detail** | Texture, sign, structure for ID |
| **Metadata** | Date, time, place, direction, conditions |
| **Field note** | What you noticed; what you don't know |
| **Ethics** | Distance, trampling, baiting, honesty in post-processing |

**Lessons must include:**

- When *not* to photograph (nesting, stress, private land, sacred sites)  
- How atmosphere in a frame relates to real weather (Scenes)  
- How to pair a gallery image with a Field Note and a lesson  
- Comparison pairs: same place, different season; same species, different age  

**Gallery plaque standard (every featured frame):**

> *[Title]* — [Location], [Season], [Year]  
> *Observation:* [One sentence of what was happening in nature]  
> *Evidence:* [Camera settings optional; conditions required]  
> *Connection:* [Link to species, lesson, or phenomenon]

## CONNECTIONS

### Every lesson explains the web

No phenomenon stands alone. Every piece of Waypoint content includes an explicit **Connections** section — prose, diagram, or map — tying the subject to as many of these spheres as honestly apply:

| Sphere | Example connections |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Geology** | Bedrock, slope, aspect, erosion, landform |
| **Weather** | Fronts, pressure, wind, microclimate |
| **Climate** | Long-term patterns, range shifts, timing |
| **Plants** | Structure, phenology, community, indicator species |
| **Fungi** | Decomposition, symbiosis, season, substrate |
| **Animals** | Behavior, sign, migration, habitat use |
| **Insects** | Pollination, decomposition, food webs |
| **Water** | Surface, groundwater, chemistry, flow |
| **Soils** | Texture, organic matter, pH, hydrology |
| **Humans** | History, land use, stewardship, indigenous knowledge (sourced respectfully) |

**Connection prompt (required in every lab):**

> *If you change one variable — temperature, water, stone, or human use — what else in this web would shift?*

Cross-link to sister products when the connection crosses domains (e.g. ForageCast species ↔ Steepleaf habitat ↔ SignalTerrain map).

## OUTDOOR CHALLENGES

### A system of invitations — not gamification

Challenges are **assignments to notice**, not streaks, points, or leaderboards. They honor the Constitution: no engagement bait.

| Cadence | Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Daily** | One small act of attention | "Find three shades of green on your walk." |
| **Weekly** | One investigation | "Photograph the same sky view each evening; note cloud type." |
| **Monthly** | One deeper theme | "Document one species weekly through the month." |
| **Seasonal** | Phenology and change | "First frost sign · first spring frog · longest day shadow." |

**Challenge structure:**

```
CHALLENGE: [Name]
Cadence: Daily | Weekly | Monthly | Seasonal
Observe: [What to notice]
Investigate: [Optional lesson link]
Document: [Field Note or photo evidence]
Connect: [One connection question]
Reflect: [One sentence prompt]
Share: [Optional — private default]
```

**Seasonal calendar (ecosystem-wide):**

- **Spring:** arrival, bud burst, migration north, morel windows (regional)  
- **Summer:** drought stress, alpine access, night sky, fire weather awareness  
- **Autumn:** senescence, migration south, shed season, harvest  
- **Winter:** track reading, silence, stars, cold-soil dormancy  

Products surface challenges appropriate to their domain; Waypoint Studio Home may feature one cross-product challenge per week.

## CITIZEN SCIENCE

### Meaningful contribution — user in control

Waypoint Studio may invite optional observation sharing that supports education, research, and conservation. **Never trick users into sharing data.**

**Before any contribution UI exists, the user must see:**

| Disclosure | Content |
|------------|---------|
| **What** | Exact fields collected (species, date, location precision, photo, notes) |
| **Why** | Which question this helps answer |
| **How used** | Education, research partner, aggregate trends — named when possible |
| **Privacy** | Private · anonymous · public — default **private** |
| **Revoke** | How to delete or withdraw |

**Principles:**

- Opt-in only; private by default  
- Location fuzzing offered for sensitive species and personal safety  
- No selling personal observation streams  
- Credit the observer when public; allow pseudonym  
- Teach *how* observations become science — the Method, not the funnel  

**Good fit:** phenology dates, range extensions, invasive reports, water clarity, night sky brightness, ethically documented wildlife sign.

**Poor fit:** gamified collection, mandatory account to learn, vague "help science" without naming the use.

## USER EXPERIENCE

### Every page answers these six questions

| Question | Visitor need |
|----------|----------------|
| **What am I looking at?** | Orient — name the phenomenon, species, place, or frame |
| **Why does it happen?** | Mechanism — process, cause, season, geology |
| **How is it connected?** | Web — required Connections section |
| **What can I investigate today?** | Outdoor investigation or challenge — actionable now |
| **What tool can help me?** | Workshop link — secondary, honest about limits |
| **What should I observe outside?** | Field observations — senses, site, repeat visit |

If a page answers only the first two and stops, it is a textbook — not Waypoint.

If a page answers only the tool question, it is software — not Waypoint.

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Warm** | Parchment, hearth light, welcome |
| **Cozy** | Cabin, not convention center |
| **Traditional** | Field guide, museum, visitor center — not trend-chasing UI |
| **Educational** | Every screen teaches something |
| **Nature first** | Photograph, season, place before chrome |
| **Photography first** | Evidence and beauty from the real world |
| **Illustrations second** | Plates and diagrams where photos cannot teach |
| **Technology supports learning** | Tools serve labs — never the reverse |

**Never:** social feeds, dashboards, autoplay, gamification, clickbait, dark patterns, enterprise chrome.

**Authority chain:** Constitution → Waypoint Method → WEF schema → product content.
